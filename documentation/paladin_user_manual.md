# Getting Started

## How to Register

1. Click **Sign In** in the top right of the screen
2. Click **Sign in with Google**
3. Enter your email address
4. Enter your password if prompted
5. On the Registration page, fill in the following tabs:
   - **Profile** — your name, phone number, email, and bio
   - **Farm Details** — farm name, address, location on the map, acreage, gates
   - **Crops** — list your crops and acreage (or select "No Crops")
   - **Livestock** — list your animals and count (or select "No Livestock")
   - **Equipment** — list available equipment (or select "No Equipment")
   - **Emergency Needs** — list any standing emergency needs (or select "No Needs")

After completing registration, you'll be redirected to the Dashboard.

## How to Edit Your Profile

1. Select your **profile icon** in the top right of the screen (the circle showing your Google account picture)
2. From the menu that appears, select **Edit Profile**
3. Navigate through the tabs to change any information you'd like to update
4. You may also add, update, and delete other farms if you have more than one
5. When complete, click the **Save Changes** button at the bottom of the screen

> **Note:** Only one farm may be modified, added, or deleted at a time.

## Adding a New Farm

From the profile editing page, click the dropdown in the top left, then click the **Add Farm** button. Then, fill in the details just like during registration.

## Deleting a Farm

Again from the profile editing page, click the dropdown in the top left. Select the farm you want to remove and click the **Delete Farm** button. You'll be asked to confirm before the farm is permanently deleted.

## Switching Themes

1. Select your **profile icon** in the top right of the screen (the circle showing your Google account picture)
2. In the menu that appears, you will see either **Light Mode** or **Dark Mode** depending on your current setting
3. Click on this menu item to switch to the other option

Your preference is saved automatically and will persist across sessions.

## Donating

You can support Paladin Farm & Ranch through PayPal donations. The donate option appears:

- On the **landing page** via the "Get Involved" section
- After completing **registration** (a donate dialog appears automatically)

### Monthly Subscription

1. Click **Donate**
2. Choose the **Subscribe** option to set up a recurring monthly donation through PayPal

> **Note:** An active monthly subscription is required for regular users to create disaster requests. Admins and Staff are exempt from this requirement.

## Push Notifications

A **bell icon** appears in the bottom-left corner of every page when you're signed in.

- Click it to **subscribe** to browser push notifications
- Click again to **unsubscribe**

When subscribed, you'll receive a browser notification whenever a new disaster request is created on the platform.

> **Note:** Your browser will ask for permission the first time. Push notifications require a modern browser that supports the Web Push API.

## Email Notifications

The platform sends automated emails for key events:

| Event                            | Who receives it                    |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Request created                  | The request creator (confirmation) |
| Someone responds to your request | The request owner                  |
| A response is cancelled          | The request owner                  |
| Request is closed                | The request owner + all responders |
| Organization approved/rejected   | The requester                      |
| Join request submitted           | Organization managers              |
| Contact message sent             | The farm owner or org owner        |

Emails are sent automatically to the profile you signed up with. You can opt out of most email notifications by turning off **Email Notifications** in your profile settings (click **Edit Profile** from your profile page). Note that direct contact messages will still be delivered regardless of this setting.

# Dashboard

## Map View

The Dashboard centers on an interactive Google Map showing your farms (green markers) and active disaster requests (red markers). The map auto-centers on your farms. If you have no farms registered yet, the map falls back to your current location (if location sharing is enabled) or a broad view of the United States.

> **Tip:** Your browser will prompt you to allow location sharing the first time you open the Dashboard. Allowing it helps the map center on nearby requests and farms.

- **Pan and zoom** to discover nearby requests and community farms
- Click any **marker** to see details or contact the owner
- Use **"All Requests"** or **"All Farms"** links at the top to zoom out and show everything

## Sidebar Tabs

The left sidebar has three tabs:

| Tab         | Shows                                                                            |
| ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Yours**   | Your own requests, filterable by Active / Inactive / All                         |
| **Members** | Active requests from your organization members' farms                            |
| **Nearby**  | Nearby active requests visible on the current view of the map (with count badge) |

Click any request to open its detail panel with farm info, responders, and actions.

## Organization Switching

If you belong to multiple organizations, use the **dropdown** at the top of the left sidebar to switch between them. This changes which members' requests appear in the **Members** tab and allows owners/managers to manageme members in the sidebar.

From the sidebar you can also access:

- **Members** — view and manage org members (Owners/Managers can contact them via email here as well)
- **Join Requests** — handle pending join requests (Owners/Managers only)
- **Contact Owner** — reach out to the owner of the organization

## How to Create a Request

1. Click the **New Request** button in the navigation bar at the top of the page
2. Select the **type of disaster** (fire, flood, hurricane, tornado, drought, frost, or other)
3. Select the **farm** affected by the disaster
4. _(Optional)_ Add comments in the **description** with additional details about the situation
5. _(Optional)_ Check **"Do you have a Gate you want to add?"** and select a gate from the dropdown — this tells responders which entrance to use
6. Click **Create Request**

After creating the request:

- You'll be redirected to the dashboard, centered on this request
- It will appear on the map as a marker
- All push notification subscribers will be notified
- You'll receive a confirmation email

## How to View Your Requests

1. Navigate to the **Dashboard** using the Dashboard button in the navigation bar at the top of the page
2. Your open requests will appear in the **"Yours"** tab on the left side of the screen
3. Click on a request to view its full details, including responders and farm information

You can filter your requests by status using the filter at the top: **Active**, **All**, or **Inactive**.

## How to View All Active Requests

1. From the Dashboard, switch to the **"Nearby"** tab to see active requests from other farmers
2. Click on a request to view its full details

If the map area doesn't show any requests, click **"All Requests"** at the top of the sidebar to load and zoom to fit all active requests on the map.

## How to Respond to a Request

1. Navigate to the **Dashboard**
2. Find the request you want to respond to (see [View Requests](#how-to-view-your-requests) for help)
3. Click on the request to open its details
4. Click **Respond to Request**
5. Enter your **Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA)**
6. (Optional) List any **equipment** you will be bringing, or other notes
7. Click **Submit Response**

After responding:

- The request owner will be notified by email
- Your response will appear in the request's responders list with your ETA and equipment details

## Cancelling Your Response

If you need to withdraw, open the request details, expand your response entry, and click **Cancel My Response**.

## How to Close a Request

1. Navigate to the **Dashboard**
2. Open the **"Yours"** tab to find your request
3. Click on the request to view its details
4. Scroll to the bottom and click **Close Request**
5. Confirm when prompted — this action cannot be undone

After closing:

- The request will be marked as inactive
- All responders will be notified by email that assistance is no longer needed
- You'll receive a confirmation email

For more about email notifications, see [Notifications](#push-notifications).

# Organizations

## How to Join an Organization

1. Navigate to the **Organizations** page using the **Orgs** link in the navigation bar at the top of the page
2. Click the **Join Organization** tab
3. Use the search bar to find an organization by name
4. Click **Request to Join** on the organization you want to join
5. _(Optional)_ Add a message to your join request
6. Click **Send Request**

Your request will be reviewed by the organization's owner or manager. You'll see a **"Pending"** badge until it's approved.

Once approved, the organization will appear in your **My Organizations** tab and its members' requests will be visible in your Dashboard.

## How to Request a New Organization

1. Navigate to the **Organizations** page using the **Orgs** link in the navigation bar at the top of the page
2. Click the **Create Organization** tab
3. Enter a **name** for your organization
4. Enter a brief **description** explaining the organization's purpose
5. Click **Submit Request**

Your request will be reviewed by a platform administrator. You'll be notified by email when it's approved or rejected.

Once approved, you'll automatically be set as the **Owner** of the new organization and can begin inviting members.

> **Note:** You can only have one pending organization request at a time.

## Member Roles

Organizations have three roles:

| Role        | Permissions                                                                                     |
| ----------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Owner**   | Full control — manage members and their roles, approve/reject requests, delete the organization |
| **Manager** | Can manage members and approve/reject join requests                                             |
| **Member**  | Can view organization members' requests on the Dashboard                                        |

> **Note:** Only Owners can promote other members to Owner. There must always be at least one Owner in an organization.

## Adding Members

Owners and Managers can add members directly by email.

1. Navigate to the **Dashboard**
2. Select your organization from the **dropdown** at the top of the sidebar
3. Click **Members** in the sidebar
4. Enter the **email address** of the person you want to add
5. Select a **Role**
6. Click **Add**

The new member will be added with the **Member** role by default.

> **Note:** The person must already have a Paladin account. If they haven't registered yet, ask them to sign up first, or they can [request to join](#how-to-join-an-organization) your organization themselves.

## Handling Join Requests

When someone requests to join your organization, Owners and Managers can approve or reject the request.

### From the Dashboard

1. Navigate to the **Dashboard**
2. Select your organization from the dropdown
3. Click **Join Requests** in the sidebar — a red badge shows the count of pending requests
4. Review each request (you'll see the requester's name and optional message)
5. Click **Approve** or **Reject**

### From the Organizations Page

1. Navigate to **Organizations** (Orgs) page
2. Click the **Member Requests** tab
3. Review and approve/reject pending requests

Approved users will be added as **Members** to your organization.

## Changing a Member's Role

1. Navigate to the **Dashboard**
2. Select your organization from the dropdown
3. Click **Members** in the sidebar
4. Find the member whose role you want to change
5. Use the **role dropdown** next to their name to select Owner, Manager, or Member

## Contacting a Farm Owner

From the Dashboard map, you can contact a farm owner when they have an active request visible to you.

1. Click on a **request marker** on the map
2. In the info popup, click **Contact Owner**
3. Enter your message
4. Click **Send**

The farm owner will receive your message via email.

> **Note:** If there is no active request, you will only see the farm name and city/state on the map.

## Contacting an Organization Owner

1. Navigate to the **Organizations** page
2. Find the organization in the **My Organizations** tab
3. Click the **Contact Owner** option on the organization card
4. Enter your message and send

The organization's owner will receive your message via email.

## Emailing Organization Members

Admins, Owners, and Managers can email members directly from the Dashboard.

1. Navigate to the **Dashboard**
2. Select your organization from the dropdown
3. Click **Members** in the sidebar
4. Click the **email icon** next to a member's email address to open your mail client

## Emailing a User (Admin Only)

1. Click **Users** in the navigation bar at the top of the page
2. Find the user in the data table
3. Click the **email icon** next to their email address to open your mail client

## Leaving an Organization

1. Navigate to the **Organizations** page
2. In the **My Organizations** tab, click the **…** (three-dot) menu on the organization card
3. Click **Leave Organization**

> **Note:** If you are the last Owner, you must first transfer ownership by [changing another member's role](#member-roles) to Owner before you can leave. Alternatively, you can choose to delete the organization.

## Deleting an Organization

1. Navigate to the **Organizations** page
2. In the **My Organizations** tab, click the **…** (three-dot) menu on the organization card
3. Click **Delete Organization**
4. Confirm when prompted — this permanently removes the organization and all memberships

# Admin

## User Management

Admins have access to a dedicated user management page at `/users`.

### Viewing Users

1. Click **Users** in the navigation bar at the top of the page (visible only to Admins)
2. A data table shows all registered users with columns for name, email, role, and subscription status
3. Use the column headers to **sort** or **filter** the list

### Changing a User's Role

1. Click on the **Role** cell for any user in the table
2. Select a new role from the dropdown: **Admin**, **Staff**, or blank (regular user)
3. The change saves automatically

### Editing User Details

1. **Double-click** any cell in the table to make it editable
2. Make your changes
3. Click the **Save** button that appears to confirm, or **Discard** to cancel

### Contacting a User

You can email any user directly from the table — see [Contact Members](#emailing-a-user-admin-only) for details.

## Disaster Resources

Admins can manage the disaster resources shown on the public **Resources** page.

### Adding a Resource

1. Navigate to **Resources** in the navigation bar at the top of the page
2. Click the **Add Resource** button
3. Fill in the **name**, **URL link**, and optional **description**
4. Click **Save**

### Editing a Resource

1. Find the resource in the list
2. Click the **Edit** button on the resource card
3. Update the fields and click **Save**

### Deleting a Resource

1. Click the **Delete** button on the resource card
2. Confirm the deletion when prompted

## Organization Request Review

When users request to create a new organization, it goes into a review queue visible only to Admins.

### Reviewing Requests

1. Navigate to the **Organizations** page
2. Click the **Org Requests** tab (visible only to Admins)
3. You'll see all pending, approved, and rejected requests

### Approving a Request

1. Review the organization name and description
2. _(Optional)_ Add **review notes**
3. Click **Approve**

The organization will be created and the requester will become its **Owner**. They'll receive an email notification.

### Rejecting a Request

1. _(Optional)_ Add **review notes** explaining the reason
2. Click **Reject**

The requester will be notified by email with your notes.
